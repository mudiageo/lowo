# Lowo Demo Voice-Over Script
**Target duration: 2–4 minutes | 6 scenes**

---

## Scene 1 — Dashboard (0:00 – 0:30)
*Open the app on a mobile-sized viewport. The Dashboard is visible.*

> "Meet **Lowo** — a premium, offline-first budget planner built specifically for the Nigerian reality. The moment you open the app, you're greeted by a live dashboard showing your active budget period at a glance.
>
> Here you can see the period name, its exact date range, and how many days are left. Below that — the total amount spent, a real-time progress bar, and a breakdown of your **top spending categories**, each with its own icon, colour, and percentage.
>
> No page reload needed. No waiting. All of this data lives right here on your device."

---

## Scene 2 — Quick-Add Expense via FAB (0:30 – 1:00)
*Tap the central floating action button (+) in the bottom navigation bar.*

> "Adding an expense is a single tap away. I'll press the **plus button** in the bottom nav — that's the F-A-B, the Floating Action Button — and a smooth drawer slides up from the bottom.
>
> I pick a category — let's choose *Food* — enter the amount, maybe a quick note, then hit **Save**. Done. The expense is instantly committed to IndexedDB on my device, and the dashboard updates in real time.
>
> No round trip to a server. No loading spinner. That's the power of local-first architecture."

---

## Scene 3 — Budgets & Calendar-Anchored Period (1:00 – 1:45)
*Navigate to the Budgets screen. Open the "New Budget Period" modal.*

> "Now let's look at **Budgets**. Lowo supports a concept that most budget apps ignore — the idea that for most Nigerian workers, your budget month doesn't start on the 1st; it starts on **salary day**.
>
> I'll tap **New Budget** and the creation form opens. I can give it a name — say, *March Salary Run* — and choose my period type. I'll select **Calendar-Anchored** and set the start day to the 25th, which is when my salary lands.
>
> Lowo automatically calculates the end date. I then set a total pool — my spending money — and distribute it across categories: food, transport, data, rent. Each slice has its own allocation.
>
> When I save, this period becomes active and every expense I log gets tracked against these category limits."

---

## Scene 4 — AI Insights & Personality Card (1:45 – 2:30)
*Navigate to the Insights screen. Press "Generate New Insights".*

> "This is where Lowo gets genuinely smart. The **AI Insights** screen is powered by Google Gemini — running locally from your own API key, so your financial data never leaves your device.
>
> I press **Generate New Insights** and Lowo sends your anonymised spending summary — amounts, categories, patterns — directly to the Gemini model. In seconds, it returns a personalised **Financial Personality Card**.
>
> Something like: *'The Vigilant Saver — you consistently allocate ahead of impulse purchases'*. Below that, three concrete, Nigerian-context recommendations: bulk buying at the market, managing airtime spend, budgeting for power outages.
>
> This isn't generic advice. It's tailored to your actual money flow, generated fresh each time you ask."

---

## Scene 5 — Desktop View & Command Palette (2:30 – 3:00)
*Resize browser to desktop width. Press Ctrl+K (or Cmd+K on Mac).*

> "Lowo is fully responsive. Switching to a wider screen, the bottom navigation collapses and a beautiful **sidebar** takes its place — with your name, navigation links, and quick stats.
>
> Power users will love this: press **Ctrl K** and the **Command Palette** opens. You can jump to any screen, log a new expense, create a savings goal, or even toggle dark mode — all without touching the mouse.
>
> This is a productivity layer that most mobile-first budget apps simply don't offer."

---

## Scene 6 — PWA Install & Offline Capability (3:00 – 3:20)
*Show the browser's install prompt. Then turn off network in DevTools and reload.*

> "Finally — **Lowo is a Progressive Web App**. Your browser will offer to install it to your home screen, giving you a full native-app experience without an app store.
>
> Let me show you something important: I'll turn off the network connection and reload. The app still loads. The data is all there. I can still view my budget, log expenses, and navigate — completely offline.
>
> This is what *local-first* truly means. Your finances, always available, regardless of connectivity.
>
> **Lowo. Your money, your rules — all on your device.**"

---

*[End of demo — total approx. 3 min 20 sec]*
